Kokhe Danda Trek —
Wild Ridge

A hidden gem through ancient oak forests, sacred viewpoints, and ridge-top meadows — the Kokhe Danda Trek offers Himalayan solitude like no other.

The Kokhe Danda Trek is a 3 to 6 day off-the-beaten-path ridge trek in Parbat District, Nepal, reaching a maximum altitude of 3,310 metres. Situated 100 metres higher than the famous Poon Hill, Kokhe Danda offers a truly panoramic viewpoint of the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri mountain ranges — yet sees a fraction of the crowds. Pioneered by Discover Parbat, this trail was opened to share one of western Nepal's best-kept secrets with the world.

Starting from Pokhara, the trek winds through rhododendron forests, Magar village homestays, and open ridge-top meadows before arriving at the Kokhe Danda summit for breathtaking sunrise views over three eight-thousanders: Dhaulagiri, Annapurna, and Manaslu. Suitable for first-time trekkers and seasoned walkers alike, this is one of the most rewarding short treks in Nepal.

The Story Behind the Trek

A Ridge Known
to Few, Loved by All

Kokhe Danda — meaning "Corner of a hill" — has been a sacred passage for locals and herders for centuries. Weaving through sub-alpine meadows, dense rhododendron groves, and a series of panoramic viewpoints overlooking the Dhaulagiri and Annapurna massifs, this trail was designed by Discover Parbat to share one of Parbat's best-kept secrets with the world — responsibly, authentically, and unforgettably.
